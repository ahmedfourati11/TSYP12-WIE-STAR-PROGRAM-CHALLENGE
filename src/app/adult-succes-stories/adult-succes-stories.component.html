Success Stories

"The Little Inventor" impresses Moroccans with his innovations

Bilal Hamouti

At just 12 years old, Bilal Hamouti managed to create several smart devices and now has a collection of inventions, including "Safety Glasses," "the Mask of the Future" that helps prevent COVID-19 infections, the "Badri Hari Robot" which mimics hand movements, and a "Smart Trash Bin," among other innovations. Hamouti, known in Morocco as the "Young Inventor," won the Future Engineers category in the 2020 World Robotics Competition, recognizing his achievements.

"Passionate about Space from a Young Age"

Sarah Al-Amiri

Although Sarah Al-Amiri is now a prominent figure, her passion for space science began as a teenager in the United Arab Emirates. At 15, she was already dedicated to learning about technology and space exploration. She went on to play a major role in the UAE’s Mars Mission as the Deputy Project Manager of the Hope Probe, the first Arab mission to Mars. Sarah's journey from a young space enthusiast to a leader in her country's space program inspires countless Arab youth to pursue careers in STEM fields and aspire to reach the stars.

"Teen Innovator and Social Impact Leader"

Omar Al-Busaidy

Omar Al-Busaidy, a young Emirati, invented an automated pill dispenser at just 16 years old. His device was designed to help elderly patients and individuals with chronic illnesses remember to take their medication. Omar’s device received widespread recognition and awards in the UAE and beyond, including prizes in Global Innovation Competitions. He continues to develop other socially impactful projects and is dedicated to inspiring Arab youth to use technology to improve people’s lives.

"Young Emirati Inventor more short"

Adeeb Al-Blooshi

Adeeb Al-Blooshi, a young inventor from the UAE, gained attention at just 10 years old for creating a waterproof prosthetic leg for his father. His curiosity led him to develop other innovations, including a smart seatbelt and a robotic vacuum cleaner. Adeeb's work earned him recognition as one of the youngest Arab inventors, and he was named an Arab Hope Maker for his contributions to innovation and his community. His story inspires young Arabs to pursue creativity and problem-solving in STEM fields.
